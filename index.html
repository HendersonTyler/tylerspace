About Tyler

I graduated from the University of Southern Queensland in 2011 with a Bachelor of Applied Media with a minor in marketing. I originally attended the school to pursue a career in radio. I worked at 90.9 Sea FM on the Gold Coast starting as a radio panel operator for the local station and sending national shows such as Hamish and Andy and Fifi and Jules to 50 other regional stations across the country. I progressed to becoming an overnights and weekends radio announcer locally and across Australia. In 2016 I moved to Canberra and became the afternoons' announcer on Hit 104.7, the number 1 Canberra radio station. While in radio I won an Australian Commerical Radio Award.

Through connections made at the radio station, I was able to freelance as a camera operator and video editor. I travelled to Kangaroo Island and scuba dived the Great Barrier Reef as a cameraman for a Network 7 travel show. I was able to keep working as a freelance video editor, travelling through Europe, Asia and North America for 9 months in 2019.

I have returned to Australia, am looking to settle down, and am ready for a career change. I studied HTML and CSS at University, and ever since have built little websites for friends as well as my side projects. I have completed a full-stack web development course and am looking to join the workforce as a developer.

Who I'd like to meet

I am a fast, self-taught learner. I don't know everything, and don't pretend to. I'm looking to start somewhere that will give me room to grow.
